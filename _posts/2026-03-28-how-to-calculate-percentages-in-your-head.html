You're staring at a restaurant bill, trying to calculate a 15% tip. Or maybe you're at a store, wondering how much you'll actually pay for a jacket that's "20% off." Instead of reaching for your phone, try these simple mental math tricks to look like a genius.

1. The 10% Decimal Move

Finding 10% of any number is the foundation of mental math. All you have to do is move the decimal point one place to the left.

  • 10% of $45.00 = $4.50
  • 10% of 320 = 32

2. The 5% Trick (Half of 10%)

Once you know how to find 10%, finding 5% is a breeze. Just find 10% and divide that number in half.

Let's say your dinner bill is $60 and you want to leave a 15% tip:

  1. Find 10% of $60 = $6
  2. Find 5% (half of 6) = $3
  3. Add them together (10% + 5%) = $9 tip.

3. The Reversible Percentage Trick (Mind Blown 🤯)

Did you know that percentages are reversible? X% of Y is exactly the same as Y% of X.

For example, if someone asks you to calculate 4% of 75 in your head, you might freeze. But if you reverse it to 75% of 4, it's instantly obvious. 75% is three-quarters, and three-quarters of 4 is simply 3.

4. The 20% Discount Trick

If you see a sign that says "20% off", finding the discount is easy: just find 10% (by moving the decimal) and double it.

If a TV costs $400 and is 20% off: 10% is $40. Double it to get $80. Your discount is $80.

When the Math Gets Complicated...

Mental math is great for simple everyday situations. But when you are dealing with odd numbers, exact taxes, or complex business margins, precision is key.
